# Chemical Trading Strategy Skill

## Purpose
Guide business-facing decisions: supplier negotiation, pricing, RFQ handling, and compliance for industrial chemical exports.

## Supplier Management

### Relationship Strategy with Manufacturers
- **Primary Partners**: GACL, Grasim, Reliance, DCM Shriram, Kutch producers.
- **Engagement Model**:
  - Maintain quarterly check-ins; build long-term relationships.
  - Request volume discounts for aggregated bulk orders.
  - Ensure COA/QA support for each batch.
  - Align with their export timelines (avoid rush charges).

### Quality Assurance Checklist
- ✓ Verify COA (Certificate of Analysis) matches specs.
- ✓ Request MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet) for buyer's safety.
- ✓ Confirm packaging format (drums, containers, ISO tanks).
- ✓ Cross-check HS codes (Harmonized System) for export classification.
- ✓ Validate manufacturer batch traceability.

## Pricing Intelligence

### Cost Components (FOB India)
1. **Manufacturing cost** (50–60%): GACL/Grasim list price.
2. **Logistics to port** (5–10%): Hazira, Mundra, JNPT.
3. **Documentation & compliance** (2–3%): COA, MSDS, export filing, customs.
4. **Operating margin** (5–10%): Jaydev overhead + profit.

### Pricing Strategy
- **Volume Discounts**: 25–50 MT, 50–100 MT, 100+ MT tiers.
- **Market Sensitivity**: Track commodity prices (sulfuric acid, caustic soda are commodity chemicals; price fluctuates monthly).
- **Competitive Positioning**: Undercut by 5–10% vs. larger traders, but never compete on cost alone.
- **Incoterms Flexibility**: FOB (buyer arranges shipping), CIF (Jaydev arranges), CFR, EXW as needed.

### Margin Targets
- **Standard product (Caustic, H2O2, Sulph. Acid)**: 5–8% FOB margin.
- **Specialty/import products (Lauric Acid, Zircon Sand)**: 10–15% margin.
- **Volume deals (100+ MT)**: Negotiate lower margin but higher absolute profit.

## RFQ Handling Workflow

### Inquiry Reception
1. **Initial Email/WhatsApp**: Buyer submits product, qty, destination, timeline.
2. **Qualification Check**:
   - Is destination a known market? (Africa, GCC, SE Asia – good. Random country – clarify.)
   - Is qty realistic? (25 MT minimum; if <25 MT, consider surcharge or bundle with other products.)
   - Is timeline feasible? (If "immediate shipment" – manage expectations; standard lead is 2–4 weeks FOB India.)

### Quote Preparation (24–48 hrs)
- **Product Spec**: Confirm grade, purity, packaging format.
- **Quantity & MOQ**: State minimum order qty; offer bulk discounts if applicable.
- **Pricing**: Quote FOB Hazira/Mundra (specify port).
- **Incoterms**: Offer FOB standard; mention CIF available on request.
- **Shipping Timeline**: State typical lead time to destination (e.g., "FOB Hazira, 15 days to Dar es Salaam port").
- **Payment Terms**: Offer LC primary; mention TT/DA for established buyers.
- **COA/Docs**: State that full COA, MSDS, B/L, CoO will be provided pre-shipment.
- **Validity**: Quote valid for 7–14 days.

### Order Confirmation
- Once buyer accepts, issue **Pro Forma Invoice** with all terms.
- Request **Purchase Order** from buyer (for their records).
- Confirm **shipping port** and timeline with manufacturer.
- Arrange **LC or payment** per agreed terms.

### Pre-Shipment Coordination
1. Request **COA + MSDS** from manufacturer.
2. Arrange **pre-shipment inspection** (if buyer requests).
3. Prepare **packing list, shipping marks** (clearly labeled with buyer's PO number).
4. Coordinate with **freight forwarder** for container booking.
5. Ensure **customs filing** (IEC-based export declaration).

### Post-Shipment
- Provide **B/L (Bill of Lading)**, CoO (Certificate of Origin), other shipping docs.
- Send **shipment tracking** link to buyer.
- Request **delivery confirmation** upon arrival.
- Follow up for **feedback** and repeat order prospects.

## Compliance & Documentation

### Export Requirements
- **IEC Code**: Jaydev's IEC (AAKCM4483G) must appear on all export docs.
- **GST Invoice**: If buyer is registered in India (B2B), invoice includes GST.
- **GSTR-1**: Report exports in GST return.
- **Customs Declaration**: File export duty, HS code, quantity, value at port.

### Shipping Documents
1. **Invoice** (Commercial Invoice, showing FOB price).
2. **Bill of Lading (B/L)**: Proof of shipment (FCL or LCL).
3. **Certificate of Origin (CoO)**: Confirms "Made in India" origin (required by many buyers & import regulations).
4. **Packing List**: Itemizes contents (weight, packaging, marking).
5. **Certificate of Analysis (COA)**: Product specs verified by manufacturer.
6. **MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet)**: Safety info for hazardous chemicals.

### Incoterms Quick Reference
- **FOB (Free on Board Hazira)**: Buyer pays for shipping & insurance. Jaydev handles port loading.
- **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)**: Jaydev arranges & pays for shipping. Buyer assumes risk at destination.
- **CFR**: Jaydev pays freight, buyer pays insurance.
- **EXW (Ex Works)**: Buyer arranges everything; Jaydev sells from factory door (rare for Jaydev; usually FOB).

## Negotiation Tactics

### For Buyers (Discount Negotiation)
- **Volume Play**: "If we commit 100 MT per quarter, can you offer better pricing?"
- **Long-term Contract**: "Guaranteed supply agreements lead to discounts."
- **Bundle Strategy**: "If we add [Product Y] to order, can we bundle at discount?"

### For Manufacturers (Cost Reduction)
- **Aggregation**: "We can consolidate orders from multiple buyers; can you discount 10–15%?"
- **Forward Booking**: "If we commit 2–3 quarters in advance, can you lock in price?"
- **Exclusivity**: "Can we negotiate exclusivity for Africa region in exchange for volume commitment?"

## Red Flags

⚠ **Watch Out For:**
- Buyers with no documented business history (credit risk).
- Extremely low-price inquiries (might be scam or dumping).
- Vague destination or payment terms (hard to execute).
- Requests for cash-on-delivery in remote markets (shipping risk).
- Repeated quote requests without follow-up (browsing, not serious).
