## Marketing Rules

### Copy Tone
- **Professional but Approachable**: Avoid stiff corporate speak; speak to buyer needs (reliability, volume, speed, docs).
- **Export-Focused**: Always emphasize our export credentials and manufacturer backing.
- **Evidence-Based**: Reference specific manufacturers, certifications, timelines. No vague claims.
- **Action-Oriented**: CTAs should be clear and simple ("Get Quote", "Download Catalog", "WhatsApp Us").

### Product Positioning
- **Never position as a trader or middleman**: Always mention the manufacturer source.
- Example ✓: "Sourced directly from GACL – India's leading caustic producer."
- Example ✗: "We source from various suppliers."
- **Always tie to end-use**: "Caustic Soda for textile mercerizing, mining, and alkali-based industries."

### Pricing & Terms Language
- Emphasize **competitive FOB/CIF pricing** (not cheap, but fair and volume-driven).
- Mention **flexible payment options**: LC, TT, DA/DP, terms for established buyers.
- Never undercut on quality; focus on value and logistics efficiency.

### Trust Elements
- **Certifications**: IEC Code, GST Available, Manufacturer COAs.
- **Proof**: Years in business, countries served, repeat clients (anonymized if needed).
- **Speed**: "24–48 hour quote turnaround", "Timely shipments to 30+ countries".
- **Responsiveness**: WhatsApp, email, phone (with person's name and title, if possible).

## RFQ Form Strategy

### Objectives
- Capture: Product selection, quantity, destination, delivery window, buyer contact.
- Qualify: High-likelihood buyers (volume, region match, timeline realistic).
- Follow-up: Auto-email + WhatsApp notification to sales team.

### Form Fields (Minimum)
1. Company Name
2. Contact Person (Name & Title)
3. Email & Phone
4. Product(s) Required
5. Quantity (in MT)
6. Destination Port/Country
7. Delivery Window (e.g., "Within 4 weeks")
8. Special Requirements (grades, packaging, certifications)
9. Preferred Payment Terms (optional)

### Success Criteria
- Submission should trigger immediate auto-response (email).
- Sales team gets WhatsApp alert + form data.
- Quote turnaround within 24 hrs (even if "pending confirmation").

## Competitor Context

### Threats
- Larger multinational trading houses with brand recognition.
- Direct manufacturer sales (bypass middlemen).
- Cheaper Chinese suppliers (lower quality/compliance risk).

### Our Differentiation
- **Not a Chinese supplier** (Made in India, transparent sourcing).
- **Not a large conglomerate** (agile, personal touch, responsive).
- **Not a pure trader** (manufacturer-backed, quality control, export expertise).
- **Position as the "sweet spot"**: Small enough to care, large enough to deliver volume, backed by India's best manufacturers.

## Do's & Don'ts

**Do:**
- ✓ Mention specific manufacturers (GACL, Grasim) by name.
- ✓ Include certifications and compliance details (COA, MSDS, export docs).
- ✓ Emphasize "Export-ready" capabilities and timelines.
- ✓ Use professional product photography or diagrams.
- ✓ Highlight WhatsApp/responsive communication.

**Don't:**
- ✗ Oversell ("We're the best in the world" – no proof).
- ✗ Use generic stock photos of unrelated industries.
- ✗ Hide pricing or payment terms (transparency builds trust).
- ✗ Make vague claims without manufacturer backing.
- ✗ Forget the RFQ form or bury the CTA.
